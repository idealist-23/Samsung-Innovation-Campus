### 1. Check the Enterprise Master Services Agreement (MSA)

Enterprise plans usually don't follow the standard self-serve refund policy found on the website. Instead, they are governed by a custom **Master Services Agreement (MSA)** or **Statement of Work (SOW)** negotiated between FlowNote and the client's procurement/legal team.

* *Tip:* Look into your team's CRM (like Salesforce or HubSpot) or contract management tool (like Ironclad or DocuSign) to see if there is a standard Enterprise MSA template you can share, or check with your sales manager.

### 2. Locate Data Server Info

Enterprise clients ask about server locations for compliance (like GDPR or SOC 2).

* *Tip:* Check your internal Wiki (Notion, Confluence, etc.) for a **Security Compliance Package** or a **Data Processing Addendum (DPA)**. This document explicitly states whether data is hosted on AWS, Google Cloud, or Microsoft Azure, and specifies the exact regions (e.g., AWS US-East, EU-Central, etc.).
